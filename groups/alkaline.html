Alkaline Earth Metals

Alkaline Earth metals are a group of reactive elements found in **Group 2** of the periodic table. They include:

  • Beryllium (Be)
  • Magnesium (Mg)
  • Calcium (Ca)
  • Strontium (Sr)
  • Barium (Ba)
  • Radium (Ra)

Characteristics of Alkaline Earth Metals

Alkaline Earth metals are known for the following characteristics:

  • Shiny, silvery-white, and somewhat reactive.
  • Higher melting points than alkali metals.
  • React with water, but less vigorously than alkali metals.
  • Form ionic compounds, typically with a +2 charge.
  • Used in a variety of industrial and medical applications.

Reactivity with Water

Alkaline Earth metals react with water to form hydroxides and release hydrogen gas, but the reaction is less intense than alkali metals:

Applications of Alkaline Earth Metals

  • Magnesium: Used in the aerospace industry, automotive parts, and as a reducing agent in the production of titanium.
  • Calcium: Essential for bone and teeth health; also used in the construction industry in the form of cement and plaster.
  • Barium: Used in oil and gas drilling, as well as in medical imaging.

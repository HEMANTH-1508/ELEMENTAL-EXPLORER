Transition Metals

Transition metals are a group of metals found in the center of the periodic table, in **Groups 3 to 12**. They include:

  • Scandium (Sc)
  • Titanium (Ti)
  • Vanadium (V)
  • Chromium (Cr)
  • Manganese (Mn)
  • Iron (Fe)
  • Cobalt (Co)
  • Nickel (Ni)
  • Copper (Cu)
  • Zinc (Zn)
  • Silver (Ag)
  • Gold (Au)

Characteristics of Transition Metals

Transition metals are known for the following characteristics:

  • Good conductors of heat and electricity.
  • Hard, dense, and have high melting points.
  • Often form colorful compounds.
  • They can form multiple oxidation states, making them useful in a variety of chemical reactions.
  • Are widely used in industrial applications such as construction, jewelry, and electronics.

Uses of Transition Metals

  • Iron: Used extensively in construction (steel) and manufacturing.
  • Copper: Used in electrical wiring due to its excellent conductivity.
  • Gold: Used in electronics and jewelry due to its resistance to corrosion and attractive appearance.
  • Silver: Used in jewelry, silverware, and in some chemical processes.

Applications of Transition Metals

  • Nickel: Used in making stainless steel and in rechargeable batteries.
  • Cobalt: Used in producing strong magnets, batteries, and catalysts in chemical reactions.
  • Chromium: Used in chrome plating and in the production of stainless steel.

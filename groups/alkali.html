Alkali Metals

Alkali metals are a group of highly reactive elements found in **Group 1** of the periodic table. They include:

  • Lithium (Li)
  • Sodium (Na)
  • Potassium (K)
  • Rubidium (Rb)
  • Cesium (Cs)
  • Francium (Fr)

Characteristics of Alkali Metals

Alkali metals are known for the following characteristics:

  • Soft, shiny, and highly reactive.
  • Low melting and boiling points compared to other metals.
  • React vigorously with water to produce hydrogen gas and an alkaline solution.
  • Form ionic compounds when reacting with nonmetals.
  • Used in batteries, industrial chemicals, and various other applications.

Applications of Alkali Metals

  • Lithium: Used in rechargeable batteries for phones, laptops, and electric vehicles.
  • Sodium: Used in streetlights, as a heat exchanger, and in chemical industries.
  • Potassium: Essential nutrient in fertilizers and used in certain types of glass.
